About Me

I am a Full-Stack Software Engineer with over 3 years of experience delivering scalable solutions in the legal tech sector. My expertise lies in architecting resilient .NET microservices and modernizing complex monolithic UIs, with a proven track record of migrating legacy systems to modern, maintainable stacks.

I thrive on the communication component of engineering. I view myself as a technical partner to stakeholders, working to intricately understand business goals to ensure the features I deliver are pragmatic, secure, and optimized for delivery efficiency. I am a strong advocate for "Living Documentation"—ensuring that system knowledge evolves in real-time with code to support long-term maintainability and team velocity.

Before entering software, I spent 7 years as a Chartered Structural Engineer, leading technical teams on multi-billion pound infrastructure projects. This professional foundation defines how I build software today: with a rigorous commitment to reliability, automated efficiency, and solutions that eliminate manual toil to drive measurable cost reductions.

My Approach: I believe in balancing high-level technical quality with practical delivery. Whether I am refactoring legacy code to resolve security vulnerabilities or designing greenfield microservices, my goal is always to build systems that are as robust as they are efficient.

Outside of the IDE: you’ll often find me traveling, cooking, or staying active through various sports. I especially enjoy the rush of the ski slopes and the mental challenge of a local pub quiz—pursuits that fuel my curiosity and reinforce the collaborative mindset I bring to my professional work. I believe that staying balanced and active is essential to maintaining a sharp, problem-solving edge in my engineering practice.
